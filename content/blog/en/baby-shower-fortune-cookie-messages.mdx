## What Makes a Baby Shower Fortune Cookie Message Work?

The best ones avoid two things: vague optimism ("wishing you all the best!") and sentiments so large they collapse under their own weight ("your life will never be the same"). Both are true—but neither is memorable.

What works is specific and honest. A message about the particular exhaustion and joy of early parenthood lands harder than a generic blessing. One true thing, said precisely, is worth more than a paragraph of warmth.

## How to Use These Messages

**At the shower table**: Place one printed message at each seat as a favor or game piece. Guests can read their fortunes aloud—whoever gets the funniest prediction wins a small prize.

**In favor bags**: Print on cardstock, cut to fortune size (about 2.5 × 0.5 inches), fold, and tuck into wrapped chocolates, small candles, or sealed sachets. A mix of funny and heartfelt works best so guests get variety.

**In a card**: Write one heartfelt message and one funny one—the contrast lands better than either alone. The funny one first; the genuine one last.

**As a shower activity**: Write "fortune cookie predictions" on blank slips, have guests fill them in, and seal them in an envelope to open on the baby's first birthday. Use the wisdom messages from this list as starter examples.

**Custom fortune cookies**: Order from a local bakery 2 weeks in advance, or DIY: soften plain fortune cookies in the microwave (5–7 seconds each), swap the slip, press closed, and let re-harden in 30 seconds. Work one at a time.

## FAQ

### What should I write in a baby shower fortune cookie?

Say one true, specific thing—either about the parents, about what's coming, or addressed directly to the baby. "The days are long. The years are genuinely short." lands harder than "wishing you all the best." Short and specific beats long and general every time.

### How long should a baby shower fortune cookie message be?

For slips inside cookies: 50–80 characters (8–12 words). For a card or favor tag: one to two sentences. The shorter the message, the more weight each word carries.

### Should baby shower fortune cookies be funny or heartfelt?

Both. The best approach is to include a mix—guests find it more fun to compare fortunes when they vary in tone. Use funny messages for the favor bag cookies, heartfelt messages for card inserts or gifts, and wisdom messages for the mom-to-be directly.

### How many different messages do I need for a baby shower?

For a shower of 15–25 guests, 8–12 different messages is ideal. Enough variety that guests don't open identical cookies, but not so many that the printing becomes complicated.

## How to Use AI for Custom Message Brainstorming

Now let's get practical. Here's your step-by-step guide to generating custom fortune cookie messages with AI.

### Step 1: Define Your Event Context

Before generating any messages, get clear on:
- **Event type**: Wedding, corporate, party, etc.
- **Guest count**: How many unique messages do you need?
- **Tone**: Formal, casual, humorous, sentimental?
- **Theme**: Does your event have a specific motif (rustic, glamorous, beach, etc.)?
- **Must-avoid topics**: Any subjects that would be inappropriate for your guests?

### Step 2: Generate a Base Set

Head to our [AI fortune generator](/ai-generator) and:
1. Select the theme that best matches your event (Love, Inspirational, Wisdom, etc.)
2. Generate 20-30 fortunes to create a starting pool
3. Copy your favorites to a document

<Callout type="tip" title="Quantity Matters">
For a 100-guest event, aim to generate 100-150 unique messages, then curate down to your final 100. Having extras lets you drop any that don't quite fit.
</Callout>

### Step 3: Customize and Curate

AI-generated messages are excellent starting points, but the magic happens in customization:
- **Add names**: "Sarah and Michael wish you love and laughter"
- **Include dates**: "May 15, 2025: The day two families became one"
- **Reference inside jokes**: If appropriate, personalize a few for close friends
- **Match your voice**: Adjust phrasing to sound like you, not a generic greeting card

### Step 4: Balance the Collection

A good fortune cookie set has variety:
- 60% inspirational/heartfelt
- 20% humorous/playful
- 15% practical wisdom
- 5% personalized (names, dates, references)

### Step 5: Format for Printing

Standard fortune slip dimensions:
- **Width**: 2-3 inches (5-7.5 cm)
- **Height**: 0.5 inch (1.25 cm)
- **Font**: 8-10 pt, legible serif or sans-serif
- **Margins**: Keep text centered with breathing room

## DIY vs. Professional: Printing and Assembly Options

You've got your messages. Now how do you actually get them into cookies?

### Option A: Order Custom Fortune Cookies (Recommended)

Several companies specialize in custom fortune cookies:
- Submit your messages in advance
- Choose cookie flavors (vanilla, chocolate, strawberry)
- Select wrapping options
- Receive ready-to-serve cookies

**Best for**: Large events (50+ guests), couples/planners without craft time, professional finish desired.

### Option B: DIY Message Insertion

For the crafty and ambitious:
1. Purchase plain fortune cookies (available in bulk online)
2. Microwave cookies briefly (5-10 seconds) to make them pliable
3. Carefully remove existing fortune
4. Insert your custom message
5. Let cookie cool to retain shape

**Best for**: Small events (<30 guests), craft enthusiasts, last-minute customization needs.

<Callout type="warning" title="DIY Reality Check">
DIY fortune cookie customization is time-intensive. Plan for 2-3 minutes per cookie minimum. For 100 cookies, that's 3-5 hours of work. Factor this into your decision.
</Callout>

### Option C: Hybrid Approach

Order plain fortune cookies from a bakery or supplier, then insert your own printed messages. This gives you professional-quality cookies with custom messages, often at lower cost than full-custom orders.

## Making Fortune Cookies Part of Your Event Experience

Fortune cookies aren't just favors—they're experiences. Here's how to maximize their impact:

### Table Placement
Place one cookie at each seat. Guests discover them naturally and the reveal becomes part of the dinner conversation.

### Dessert Station
Create a fortune cookie bar with different flavors. Guests can choose their cookie, adding an element of selection and anticipation.

### Exit Gifts
Hand out fortune cookies as guests leave. The message serves as a final memory of the evening.

### Photo Opportunity
Set up a display where guests can crack open cookies and pose with their fortunes. Perfect for social media moments.

### Reading Ritual
At key moments (toasts, cake cutting), invite a few guests to read their fortunes aloud. Creates shared laughter and connection.
